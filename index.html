Sentiment Analysis Using Voting Classification Algorithms and Neural Network

Abhinav Gupta, Panshul Jindal, Raunak Shukla, Hemprasad Yashwant Patil

In this work, we have employed a unique dataset of 50,000 review entries to perform sentiment analysis using a variety of machine learning classification models. Before any algorithm is applied to the dataset, extensive preprocessing is performed in order to remove extraneous terms from the reviews. To get the best results, we explored a variety of classification techniques, including Naive Bayes, Support Vector Machine (SVM), Random Forest, Logistic Regression, and Extra Tree Classifier.

To combine the output from all of the underlying algorithms, all of the mechanisms are then run through a voting classification algorithm. In order to make a clear contrast, we also designed a feed-forward neural network engine on the side. The data in the neural network is optimised as it moves through different levels to get the best results.

July'22 - September'22

Total Revenue Prediction of a Sports Management Application: Grook Using Machine Learning Models

Raunak Shukla, Panshul Jindal, Abhinav Gupta, Hemprasad Yashwant Patil

Grook is a sports management app that provides a solution for people to book sports grounds, courts, and other facilities in their neighborhood for particular time slots to ensure a free space to play. It allows the owners to manage their facilities more efficiently. In this study, we have trained our model to predict the total revenue generated on a facility using ensemble Machine Learning Techniques in Regression and Classification. In addition to the existing features in the dataset like sports, days, months, etc., we have also added our polynomial features.

We’ve tested algorithms including Simple, Polynomial, Ridge, Lasso, Elastic Net Regression Techniques, and Decision Tree, Support Vector, and Stochastic Gradient Descent Regressors. To make the best prediction of total revenue generated, we have also used the voting classifier. The polynomial features have been tested over several degrees to get an even better understanding of our model’s accuracy.
