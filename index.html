AUGUST 2022 - OCTOBER 2022

Sentiment Analysis Using Voting Classification Algorithms and Neural Network

Abhinav Gupta, Panshul Jindal, Raunak Shukla, Hemprasad Yashwant Patil

Sentiment analysis is the process of contextually mining textual data to glean important subjective knowledge. I made the initiative to incorporate sentiment analysis as an additional feature to forecast consumer feedback as I continued my study on predicting maximum revenue. To guarantee the consistency of the findings, I created classification algorithms such as Naive Bayes, Random Forest, and Decision Tree. To compare the two approaches, I also created a feed-forward neural network engine. Finally, we reported an accuracy of 81% with the Neural Network Engine and 78.89% with the classification methods.

JULY 2022 - SEPTEMBER 2022

Total Revenue Prediction of a Sports Management Application: Grook Using Machine Learning Models

Raunak Shukla, Panshul Jindal, Abhinav Gupta, Hemprasad Yashwant Patil

I developed ensemble Machine Learning Regression models for this research project, including Polynomial, Ridge, Lasso, Elastic Net, and many others. I was able to predict, depending on the number of consumers, the total income a company would make on any given day. In my research, I also used the Voting Classifier, which predicts the outcome that most closely matches the majority of the findings from all the models. In conclusion, with varying degrees of polynomial characteristics, the Lasso regression model had an accuracy of 97.9%.
